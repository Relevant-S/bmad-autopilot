## Rationale (verbatim from `docs/architecture.md` lines 1453-1455)

The Story 2.9 rationale at `docs/architecture.md` lines 1445-1463 (the "Acceptance Auditor — Epic 2 Single-Layer Rationale (Story 2.9)" addendum, landed under Story 2.9 AC-8) records two clauses motivating the Acceptance Auditor as the chosen single layer at Epic 2 scope. Both clauses are reproduced verbatim below; this audit evaluates each clause against the actual three-layer composition delivered post-Stories-3.1 + 3.2 + 3.3 + 3.4.

> _From `docs/architecture.md` line 1453:_
>
> **Traceability to acceptance criteria.** The Acceptance Auditor's findings are most directly traceable to story acceptance criteria. This matches Epic 2's AC-1-only QA scope from Story 2.10 (the QA specialist at Epic 2 scope verifies AC-1 only). A single-layer minimum that lines up with AC-driven verification produces an end-to-end loop that exercises the same AC-traceability seam at both the review stage and the QA stage.

> _From `docs/architecture.md` line 1455:_
>
> **Seam-contract churn minimization at Epic 3 thickening.** The Acceptance Auditor's output shape is closest to the eventual three-layer aggregated output. Picking it as the Epic-2 layer minimizes seam-contract churn when Epic 3's Story 3.1 thickens the wrapper to all three layers — the envelope shape (`status`, `artifacts`, `findings`, `rationale`, `failed_layers`) does not change, only the wrapper's internal coverage thickens.

The contract-violation-not-silent-assumption posture at `docs/architecture.md` line 1457 is the load-bearing reason this audit exists: if either clause was invalidated by Epic 3's discoveries, Epic 3 was committed to flag the invalidation explicitly and swap to a different layer choice if needed. This artifact is the discharge of that posture.

## Per-clause assessment

### Clause (a) — Traceability to acceptance criteria

The wrapper's post-Story-3.1 Procedure section (`agents/review-bmad-wrapper.md` lines 22-30) preserves the per-layer relative AC-anchoring ordering established by the upstream `bmad-code-review` skill at `_bmad/bmm/4-implementation/bmad-code-review/steps/step-02-review.md` lines 18-29: Blind Hunter receives the diff only (NO spec, NO context docs, NO project access — AC alignment must be inferred indirectly from diff inspection); Edge Case Hunter receives the diff plus project read access (AC alignment can be inferred from diff + project context, but the AC list is not a direct input); Acceptance Auditor receives the diff plus the spec file plus loaded context docs (AC alignment is a direct input — the prompt at `step-02-review.md` line 25 explicitly says "Each finding: one-line title, which AC/constraint it violates, and evidence from the diff"). Of the three layers, only the Acceptance Auditor takes the AC list as a first-class input.

This per-layer ordering is preserved in the post-Story-3.1 Epic-3 composition: the wrapper does not reshape the per-layer input specifications, and the upstream skill's parallel-pass mechanism is composed verbatim per ADR-002 cell 5 (Specialist Wrapper Binding). The relative AC-anchoring ordering across the three layers held empirically through Stories 3.1 + 3.2 + 3.3 + 3.4. Story 2.10's AC-1-only Tier-1-evidence-only QA scope (per `_bmad-output/implementation-artifacts/2-10-minimal-qa-subagent-ac-1-only-tier-1-evidence-only.md`) is the matching seam at the QA stage: Epic 2's end-to-end loop exercises AC-traceability at both the review stage (Acceptance Auditor as the AC-direct-input layer) and the QA stage (AC-1-only verification), producing the unified AC-traceability seam Story 2.9's clause (a) named.

A counterfactual worth recording honestly: Edge Case Hunter — the "diff + project read" layer — could plausibly have produced findings traceable to ACs by inferring AC alignment from project context, but it does not receive the AC list as a direct input; AC-traceability would be indirect, and the worked example at `step-02-review.md` line 24's prompt for Acceptance Auditor (which explicitly cites "violations of acceptance criteria, deviations from spec intent, missing implementation of specified behavior, contradictions between spec constraints and actual code") would not apply. Edge Case Hunter's design intent is edge-case discovery (boundary conditions, error paths, race conditions), NOT AC verification; routing AC-traceability through it would have been a category error. The Acceptance Auditor was the right single layer for Epic 2's AC-1-only QA scope; the choice is validated.

**Clause (a) verdict: rationale-holds**

### Clause (b) — Seam-contract churn minimization at Epic 3 thickening

The five required envelope fields named in clause (b) — `status`, `artifacts`, `findings`, `rationale`, `failed_layers` — carry through the four post-Story-2.9 thickening landings unchanged in shape. The architecture.md cross-reference at line 1545 records this verbatim: "the same five required envelope fields ... carry through unchanged; orchestrator-side consumers ... require zero changes for this thickening." Empirically: Story 2.6's `validate_return_envelope`, Story 2.4's `evaluate_envelope`, and Story 2.11's `assemble_bundle` (the three orchestrator-side consumers of the wrapper's envelope contract) required ZERO changes for the 3.1 three-layer thickening — the diff for Story 3.1 touches the wrapper's prose and the harness's contract-coverage tests; it does NOT touch the orchestrator-side seam. This is the load-bearing structural witness that clause (b) holds: the seam contract is genuinely stable across the thickening.

The schema-side accommodation at Story 3.3 is the nuance the audit must address honestly. Story 3.3 added an OPTIONAL `meta` property on `$defs/finding` (single-value enum `[review-completeness]`); the wrapper's post-3.3 envelope shape is therefore not byte-for-byte identical to the wrapper's post-2.9 envelope shape — the schema is one optional field richer. Three considerations argue this is NOT seam-contract churn within the meaning of clause (b): (i) the closed-by-default `additionalProperties: false` invariant on `$defs/finding` (line 110) is preserved; the bump is a NAMED optional property, not a relaxation of the closed-by-default shape, so all Story 3.2 forward-compat loud-fail tests (rejection of `category` / `subcategory` / `tags` extras) continue to pass; (ii) the bump is additive — no existing field changed shape, no existing enum value was removed, no existing required field became optional or vice versa, so any envelope conforming to the post-2.9 schema continues to conform to the post-3.3 schema unchanged; (iii) the bump traveled through the no-introductions principle (`docs/extension-audit.md` lines 125-133) BEFORE landing in the schema, with the per-convention table row at `extension-audit.md` line 34 recording the BMAD-extension event. The seam-contract churn that clause (b) was warning against is the kind that forces orchestrator-side consumer rewrites; the Story 3.3 bump did not produce any such consumer rewrite (the orchestrator-side consumers continue to read `findings[].bucket` / `findings[].severity` exactly as before; the new `meta` field is consumed only by Story 3.4's bundle-rendering downstream and the gate at `review_layer_failure_emission_gate.py`).

A defensible alternative reading: the optional `meta` enum bump IS schema churn at the strictest interpretation of clause (b), and clause (b) should therefore be marked `partial`. This audit rejects that reading on the grounds enumerated above (additive, named, closed-by-default-preserved); however, the alternative reading is recorded here so a future re-audit can consult it. The substantive judgment is: clause (b) holds because the wrapper's CONTRACT did not break — the five required envelope fields are stable, the orchestrator-side consumers required zero changes, and the schema's closed-by-default invariant is intact.

**Clause (b) verdict: rationale-holds**

## Overall verdict

Both per-clause verdicts under AC-3 + AC-4 evaluate to `rationale-holds`. The overall judgment integrates them: the Acceptance Auditor was the right single layer for Epic-2 scope because (i) it was the only one of the three layers receiving the AC list as a direct input, aligning the review-stage AC-traceability seam with Story 2.10's AC-1-only QA scope at the QA stage; (ii) the five required envelope fields carrying through unchanged across Stories 3.1 + 3.2 + 3.3 + 3.4, combined with zero orchestrator-side consumer changes for the 3.1 thickening, demonstrates the seam-contract churn minimization in practice — the optional `meta` enum bump at Story 3.3 is additive and closed-by-default-preserving, not a contract break. The contract-violation-not-silent-assumption posture at `docs/architecture.md` line 1457 is discharged: no swap to a different layer choice is warranted; the audit serves as longitudinal evidence for downstream contributors investigating the choice.

**Verdict: rationale-holds**
